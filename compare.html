Number of Items: 5

2. How much do you trust the parliament?

Comparison Summary

Measurement Method Differences

  • Study 1 uses traditional political trust scales, focusing on trust in political institutions
  • Study 2 developed a new digital tolerance scale, focusing on tolerance attitudes in cyberspace
  • Study 3 integrated multiple measurement methods through meta-analysis, providing a more comprehensive perspective

Research Design Differences

  • Study 1 adopted a cross-national cross-sectional design with large sample size but lacks temporal dimension
  • Study 2 focused on scale development with relatively small sample but high measurement precision
  • Study 3 integrated numerous studies through meta-analysis but may have publication bias

Main Findings Differences

  • Study 1 found negative correlation between social media use and political trust
  • Study 2 found positive correlation between social media use and digital tolerance
  • Study 3 found positive correlation between social media use and political polarization
